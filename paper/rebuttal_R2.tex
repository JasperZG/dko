\point{R2.1: Insufficient 3D GNN baselines (only SchNet).}

\response Added PaiNN as second baseline: Section~5.4 \lines{427--453}, Table~5 \lines{432--449}, Appendix~L \lines{1066--1087}. PaiNN (equivariant message passing, single conformer) achieves 28\%/42\%/30\% gains on ESOL/FreeSolv/Lipo---convergence with SchNet's 21--42\% across two architecturally diverse GNNs confirms the hierarchy is robust. Justification for omitting DimeNet++/Equiformer at \lines{453}. Re: ensemble pooling on SchNet---our DKO mean ensemble baseline \lines{351} already does this and underperforms FP+XGBoost, strengthening the bottleneck claim. Abstract \lines{82}, Contribution~3 \lines{157} updated.

\point{R2.2: FreeSolv directionally inconsistent ($-13.5\%$ to $+18.0\%$).}

\response Clarified at \lines{566}: the $+18.0\%$ is a 3-seed random-split auxiliary on $N{=}642$; the definitive result is the 10-seed scaffold-split $t$-test: $-13.5\%$, $p < 3{\times}10^{-5}$ (Table~4, \lines{518}). Learning curves \lines{569--571} show benefit emerges above ${\sim}440$ molecules, explaining sensitivity at FreeSolv's small size.

\point{R2.3: Zero-padding/truncation to fixed $D$ may be the bottleneck.}

\response Feature dimension ablation \lines{484--508} (Table~6, \lines{489--504}): RMSE monotonically degrades $D{=}256 \to 2048$. If truncation were the bottleneck, larger $D$ should help---it hurts. The limitation is the curse of dimensionality, not information loss. BDE's variable dims acknowledged; adaptive encoding noted as future work \lines{508}.

\point{R2.4: Excluding pre-trained models limits taxonomy.}

\response Agreed. Taxonomy explicitly scoped to non-pre-trained setting: Related Work \lines{173}, Limitations \lines{620}. DKO statistics are complementary to pre-trained embeddings; testing this combination is future work.

\point{R2.5: Restriction to regression.}

\response Added BACE/BBBP classification: Section~5.5 \lines{461--481}, Table~7 \lines{466--479}. Conformer features hurt BACE ($-4.4\%$), no effect on BBBP ($+0.1\%$)---taxonomy confirmed for classification. Scope \lines{151}, abstract \lines{80}, Appendix~K \lines{1041--1064}.
